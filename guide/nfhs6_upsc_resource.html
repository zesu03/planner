The survey at a glance

What NFHS-6 is

Released by the Ministry of Health & Family Welfare (IIPS, Mumbai as nodal agency), NFHS-6 covered ~6.79 lakh households across 715 districts in every State and UT except Manipur. It is the first round funded entirely by the Government of India and the first since the pandemic.

Design & method

Scope
Multi-stage stratified sample with district-level estimates and urban/rural strata. Data collected via CAPI with biomarker (CAB) collection, including HIV testing. Built to inform evidence-based planning down to the district.

New this round

13 added
Fresh modules on Direct Bank Transfers, Self-Help Groups, digital and financial inclusion, and expanded clinical tests. These reflect a shift toward welfare-delivery and lifestyle-disease tracking.

The headline debate

Critique
The fact sheet shrank from 131 indicators to 101 — 43 dropped, including anaemia, child mortality, sex ratio, sanitation and clean fuel. This data-transparency question is the single most exam-worthy point in the survey.

The arc in one line

NFHS-6 shows steady gains in maternal–child health, immunisation, nutrition, financial protection and women's digital inclusion — while flagging a new generation of challenges: rising non-communicable disease, an over-medicalisation of childbirth, and the data gaps created by the dropped indicators. The full national report is still awaited, so treat disaggregated and state-level figures as provisional.

What went missing — and why it's the story

43 indicators were dropped from the fact sheet and 13 added. The omissions cut across health, vital statistics and living conditions. Hover a card to see it "redacted."

The anaemia debate

The most contested drop. NFHS-5 found roughly two-thirds of women of reproductive age anaemic. NFHS-6 reports no anaemia prevalence at all.

GovtHaemoglobin testing was suspended because the finger-prick (capillary) method overstates anaemia. Estimates will come from ICMR's Diet & Biomarkers Survey (DABS) using gold-standard venous blood.
CriticsRemoving the metric breaks the NFHS-5 trend line and weakens accountability on Anaemia Mukt Bharat. A Lancet editorial and public-health activists called it a transparency setback.

Where the data "went" instead

  • Mortality (IMR, U5MR, NMR): deferred to the Sample Registration System. NFHS-5 had IMR 35, U5MR 42, NMR 24.9 per 1,000.
  • Sex ratio at birth: dropped (NFHS-5: 929 females / 1,000 males).
  • Sanitation & clean cooking fuel: routed to Swachh Survekshan Grameen and MoSPI surveys.
  • Disability: deferred to SRS / Census.
  • FP quality, HIV knowledge, cancer screening: promised in the full national report — not yet released.

The balanced line for an answer: routing indicators to "the most authoritative source" can raise single-survey quality — but it fragments a once-comprehensive, equity-disaggregated dataset across systems that no longer allow the same household-level analysis, just as some flagship programmes face scrutiny.

Using NFHS-6 in Mains answers

Five framings that turn NFHS-6 from a data dump into an argument — each with the core point and exactly where to deploy it. Tap to open.

A1 The dual burden of malnutrition +
The argument
India is fighting undernutrition and overnutrition at once. Child stunting fell to 29.3% and severe wasting to 5.2%, yet ~1 in 3 children remains stunted and underweight barely moved (32.1%→31.8%). Adult obesity surged — women 24%→30.7% (urban 42.8%), men 22.9%→27.3% — alongside rising diabetes.
Deploy it for
Nutrition policy, NCD burden, POSHAN Abhiyaan evaluation, "double burden" questions. Pair with rising blood sugar (women 13.5%→17.8%, men 15.6%→20.9%).
A2 Data transparency & the omissions +
The argument
A national survey's value lies in comparable, disaggregated, household-level data over time. Dropping 43 indicators (131→101) — anaemia, mortality, sex ratio, sanitation — fragments this across SRS, DABS and Census, weakening equity analysis and trend continuity even if single-source quality improves.
Deploy it for
Governance, evidence-based policymaking, role of statistical systems, accountability. The strongest, most distinctive hook — lead with it.
A3 Over-medicalisation: the C-section spike +
The argument
C-sections rose 21.5%→27.2% nationally, hitting 54.1% in private facilities and ~40% in urban areas — far above WHO's 10–15% optimal band. Safe-motherhood gains coexist with unnecessary surgical risk: a regulation / market-failure story.
Deploy it for
Health-system regulation, public vs private care, maternal health quality — not just access.
A4 Women's empowerment: the digital & financial leap +
The argument
Women's internet use nearly doubled (33.3%→64.3%), own-and-use bank accounts reached 89.0%, mobile ownership 53.9%→63.6%. New DBT and SHG modules track welfare delivery. Empowerment, however, is uneven — decision-making autonomy and literacy were not refreshed in the fact sheet.
Deploy it for
GS-I society, gender, digital divide, financial inclusion (PMJDY), DBT-based welfare.
A5 Equity: the persistent gradient +
The argument
National averages hide gaps. Southern/western states (Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Goa) approach universal institutional delivery and single-digit stunting; high-burden states (Bihar, MP, Jharkhand) lag. Urban out-performs rural; caste, wealth and region still structure outcomes.
Deploy it for
Cooperative federalism, targeted vs universal schemes, regional disparity, SDG localisation. Caveat: NFHS-6 state cross-tabs aren't in the fact sheet yet — cite directionally.

Precision notes

A handful of NFHS-6 figures are routinely misquoted. Lock the exact wording so your answer scripts stay airtight.

1 · Total Fertility Rate vs the replacement level

TFR = 2.0 — which is below the replacement level of 2.1

Replacement-level fertility is 2.1, not 2.0, so India sits just beneath it — a population-stabilisation point examiners reward. The newer SRS 2024 data puts national TFR lower still, around 1.9.

2 · Breastfeeding under 6 months

~95.6% of infants under 6 months were breastfed (official framing)

The MoHFW release frames this indicator positively. If you need a feeding-gap angle instead, use a safer real figure — only about 15% of children aged 6–23 months get a minimum acceptable diet — rather than a breastfeeding "decline."

3 · High blood sugar — national vs urban

Women 17.8% national (21.9% urban) · Men 20.9% national (23.9% urban)

The urban sub-figures run several points above the national rate. Quote the national number unless you explicitly mean urban, and keep the two apart.

4 · Contraceptive prevalence labelling

69.1% is the total CPR (up from 66.7%)

The 66.7→69.1 jump is overall contraceptive prevalence, not the modern-method rate. Reliance on modern methods reportedly slipped (~56.4%→52.7%) as traditional methods rose — itself a useful family-planning critique point.

One standing caveat: the detailed national report is still awaited, so most state-level cross-tabs and several disaggregated figures aren't in the fact sheet. Cite them directionally and say so in answers.
