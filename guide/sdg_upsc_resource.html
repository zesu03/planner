The framework in brief

What the SDGs are

Adopted by all 193 UN member states in September 2015 as Transforming Our World: The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development (Resolution A/RES/70/1) — 17 goals, 169 targets, ~231 unique indicators, with 2030 as the deadline. They succeed the Millennium Development Goals (2000–2015).

The five Ps

Structure
People, Planet, Prosperity, Peace, Partnership — the SDGs integrate the three dimensions of development (economic, social, environmental) and are framed as universal, indivisible and interlinked.

The guiding principle

LNOB
Leave No One Behind. Progress must reach the furthest-behind first. Unlike the MDGs (8 goals, developing-world focus), the SDGs are universal — binding on rich and poor countries alike.

How progress is tracked

HLPF · VNR
The UN High-Level Political Forum reviews progress; countries submit Voluntary National Reviews. India submitted VNRs in 2017 and 2020. 190 of 193 states have now done a VNR.

India's machinery

NITI Aayog
NITI Aayog is the nodal body for SDG coordination and localisation. MoSPI maintains the National Indicator Framework. The SDG India Index drives competitive & cooperative federalism among states.
Two scoreboards — don't confuse them

The domestic index vs the global report

The SDG India Index (NITI Aayog, with MoSPI and the UN in India) scores India's states and UTs — national score 71/100. The Sustainable Development Report (SDSN, led by Jeffrey Sachs) ranks countries globally — India 99th of 167, score 67. Same subject, different producers, different scales: a classic Prelims trap.

India in the global Sustainable Development Report (SDSN)

The climb to 99th

India entered the global top 100 for the first time, rising 120th (2021) → 112th (2023) → 109th (2024) → 99th of 167 (2025, score 67). The 2026 edition flags India among the largest gainers worldwide. Bars show the rank improvement (shorter = better rank).

Global context: Finland leads (~87); none of the 17 goals is globally on track and only ~17% of targets are projected to be met by 2030. On the global lens India is firmly on track on just two goals — SDG 1 (No Poverty) and SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities) — while SDG 13 (Climate Action) is flagged as worsening.

The whole point — citing SDGs in answers

Where the SDGs fit your answers

Pick a common UPSC theme to see which SDGs to invoke, how to weave them in, and a ready phrase you can adapt. SDGs are the single most reusable framework across GS-I, II, III and the essay — a dependable conclusion-booster.

For GS & essay

Five framings worth memorising

A1 SDGs as the conclusion framework +
The move
Close almost any development answer by tying the issue to a specific SDG and the 2030 deadline. "Addressing X is central to meeting SDG __ and the Leave-No-One-Behind pledge." Adds direction and a global anchor without padding.
Works for
Poverty, health, education, gender, environment, governance — nearly every GS-II/III prompt and the essay.
A2 Cooperative & competitive federalism +
The argument
The SDG India Index is the textbook example of NITI Aayog using data to rank states, spread best practice and nudge laggards — competitive federalism — while pooling central-state effort on shared goals — cooperative federalism.
Works for
GS-II polity/governance, NITI Aayog's mandate, centre-state relations, evidence-based policymaking.
A3 The growth–sustainability tension +
The argument
India's rank shows GDP size and SDG performance don't move together — the US and China sit far above India on income yet face their own SDG gaps, while India's environmental goals (SDG 13 climate, SDG 12 consumption) lag its social gains. Growth must be decoupled from ecological cost.
Works for
GS-III economy/environment, green growth, Mission LiFE, just transition, the essay.
A4 Localisation & the last mile +
The argument
National averages hide district and group disparities. SDG localisation — district indices, the Aspirational Districts Programme, panchayat-level targets — operationalises Leave-No-One-Behind and is where the 2030 race is actually won or lost.
Works for
GS-II governance/welfare delivery, decentralisation, Aspirational Districts, GS-I society/regional disparity.
A5 India as a global SDG actor +
The argument
India links domestic delivery to global leadership — the International Solar Alliance, the G20 presidency's development agenda, LiFE, and South-South cooperation — positioning the 2030 Agenda within the Viksit Bharat @2047 vision.
Works for
GS-II international relations, GS-III environment/energy diplomacy, the essay.

Precision notes

The details examiners build Prelims traps around. Lock the exact attributions and figures.

1 · Two different scoreboards

SDG India Index = NITI Aayog (score 71) · Sustainable Development Report = SDSN (rank 99th, score 67)

The domestic index is produced by NITI Aayog with MoSPI and the UN in India. The global ranking is by the UN Sustainable Development Solutions Network (Jeffrey Sachs) — UN-backed, not an official UN body. Different producer, scale and purpose.

2 · 17 goals, but 16 are scored in the India Index

17 goals overall · SDG India Index scores 16 (SDG 17 assessed qualitatively)

The 4th edition covers 16 goals, 70 targets, 113 indicators. SDG 17 (Partnerships) is not given a composite score. The global SDR scores all 17.

3 · The counts to keep straight

17 goals · 169 targets · ~231 unique indicators (Global Indicator Framework)

The MDGs had 8 goals; the SDGs have 17. Targets are 169. The UN Global Indicator Framework lists 231 unique indicators. Adopted 2015, deadline 2030.

4 · The four performance bands

Aspirant 0–49 · Performer 50–64 · Front-Runner 65–99 · Achiever 100

A state at 71 is a Front-Runner, not an Achiever. "Achiever" requires a perfect 100 on that goal/composite — rare. Don't muddle the band names or cut-offs.

5 · India's "on track" goals are a global-lens figure

SDR 2025: India on track on SDG 1 & SDG 10; SDG 13 worsening

This "only two on track" reading comes from the global SDR. The domestic SDG India Index, using different indicators, records strong progress on SDG 13 and 15 — so always state which scoreboard you're citing.

Standing caveat: the SDG India Index is updated periodically (4th edition is 2023–24) and the global SDR annually, so verify the latest figures near your exam date and name the edition you quote.
